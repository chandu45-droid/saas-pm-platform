# Market Analysis — Raw Data

## Market Size
- 2025: ~$9.14–9.76 billion
- 2026: ~$10.51–11.27 billion
- 2030: ~$16.87 billion
- CAGR: 12.6–15.4% depending on segment

## Why Competitors Charge Per-Seat
1. **Predictable revenue**: Linear scaling with customer team size
2. **Higher LTV**: Revenue grows automatically as companies hire
3. **Enterprise procurement**: Fixed per-seat costs are easy to budget
4. **Network effects**: More seats = more lock-in = lower churn
5. **Industry norm**: Everyone does it, so customers expect it
6. **Sales comp alignment**: Easy to calculate expansion revenue
7. **Marginal cost justification**: Each user does consume some resources

## Why Feature Tiers Exist
1. **Price discrimination**: Extract more from those willing to pay more
2. **Upgrade path**: Creates clear sales motion (free → paid → enterprise)
3. **Enterprise justification**: Advanced security/compliance worth more
4. **Competitive moat**: Feature gaps make switching harder
5. **Revenue optimization**: Not all features cost the same to deliver

## Customer Pain Points (Per-Seat Pricing)
1. **Seat tax on growth**: Hiring more people = higher software bill
2. **Ghost seat waste**: Paying for inactive/occasional users
3. **Viewer tax**: Paying full price for read-only stakeholders
4. **Budget unpredictability**: Headcount changes = cost changes
5. **Feature hostage**: Need ONE feature in Pro? Pay 2x for everything
6. **Small team penalty**: Seat minimums and forced increments
7. **Sticker shock at scale**: 100 people × $20/seat = $2,000/mo minimum

## Customer Pain Points (Feature Tiers)
1. **Artificial limitations**: Free tier crippled to force upgrade
2. **All-or-nothing tiers**: Can't pick individual features
3. **Enterprise lock-in**: Best features only available at highest tier
4. **AI paywall**: AI features increasingly gated or add-on priced
5. **Integration tax**: Connecting other tools often requires paid tier

## Market Opportunity
- Teams 5-50 people: Most price-sensitive, least served by transparent pricing
- Startups/SMBs in emerging markets (India, SEA, LATAM): $7-20/user/mo is painful
- Project-heavy orgs (agencies, consultancies): Many occasional users
- Open-source adjacent: Teams that value transparency and fairness
- AI-native users: Want AI features without enterprise-tier pricing

## Competitive Landscape Gaps
1. No major PM tool offers true unlimited-users pricing
2. No major PM tool uses pure usage-based pricing
3. Zoho is cheapest but has weakest UX and feature set
4. ClickUp tries to be everything but is complex and buggy
5. Notion is closest to "flat" but still per-member
6. None offer transparent, real-time usage dashboards for billing
