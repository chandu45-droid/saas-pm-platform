# Executive Summary

## Opportunity
The project management software market is valued at ~$10B (2026) growing at 13-15% CAGR. Every major player (ClickUp, Jira, Asana, Monday.com, Notion, Zoho) uses per-seat pricing with feature-gated tiers — a model that punishes team growth, wastes money on inactive seats, and locks critical features behind expensive tiers.

## The Idea
Build a project management platform where:
- **All features are available to every customer** — no Basic/Pro/Enterprise tiers
- **Unlimited users** — no per-seat charges
- **Pay only for what costs real money** — storage, AI, automations, API calls
- **Transparent, real-time billing** — customers see exactly what they're paying for

## Key Findings

### The Market Gap is Real
- No major PM tool offers unlimited users with usage-based pricing
- Per-seat pricing is the #1 customer complaint across all competitors
- 37% of Fortune 5000 SaaS companies already use usage-based models successfully
- Teams in emerging markets (India, SEA) find $7-20/user/mo painful

### The Economics Work
- Infrastructure cost per active user drops from ~$5 at 100 users to ~$0.40 at 100K users
- Near-zero marginal cost for core PM actions (tasks, comments, views)
- Real costs are in storage, AI, and compute-intensive features — exactly the things to meter
- A $10/workspace/month base + metered usage model can achieve 70-80% gross margins at scale

### It's Buildable Solo
- AI-assisted development (Claude Code) enables 4-8 week MVP timeline
- Modern stack (Next.js + Supabase + Vercel) minimizes ops overhead
- Monthly infra costs start at ~$400-900 for MVP

## Recommendation: **GO** (with conditions)
The pricing model is a genuine differentiator. The market gap exists. The economics work at scale. Key conditions: must nail the core PM experience (not just pricing), need hybrid base+usage model for revenue predictability, and must target SMBs/startups first (not enterprise).
