# Go / No-Go Recommendation

## Verdict: **GO** ✅

### Confidence Level: 7.5/10

---

## Why GO

### 1. The Market Gap is Real and Defensible
- Every major PM tool uses per-seat pricing — zero exceptions
- Customer frustration with seat-based pricing is well-documented
- No one has credibly tried the "unlimited users, usage-based" model in PM
- Incumbents can't easily switch — per-seat revenue is structural to their business models

### 2. The Economics Work
- Infrastructure cost per user is $0.25-$1.50 depending on scale
- Gross margins of 88-96% at scale — comparable to or better than competitors
- Break-even at ~66-170 workspaces — achievable within 3-6 months of launch
- Revenue scales with real value delivered, not headcount

### 3. Solo Founder Feasibility is High
- AI-assisted development enables 4-8 week MVP
- Modern serverless stack (Next.js + Supabase + Vercel) minimizes ops burden
- Monthly burn of $400-900 for infrastructure at MVP stage
- Can reach ramen profitability with 200 workspaces

### 4. The Timing is Right
- 2026 is the "solo founder + AI agents" moment — this wouldn't have been feasible 2 years ago
- PM market growing 13-15% annually ($10B+)
- Developer/startup community is hungry for fair-priced tools
- Usage-based pricing is mainstream in other SaaS verticals (37% of Fortune 5000)

---

## Conditions for GO

### Must-Do (Non-Negotiable)
1. **Nail the core PM experience** — pricing alone won't carry a bad product
2. **Hybrid pricing model** — pure usage-based is too risky; need base fee for predictability
3. **Start with SMB/startup segment** — don't target enterprise at launch
4. **Real-time usage transparency** — this IS the brand, not just a feature
5. **Metering infrastructure from day 1** — retrofitting billing is hell

### Should-Do (Important)
1. Build in public — transparent pricing philosophy + transparent building process
2. Target a specific niche first (small agencies, indie dev teams, startup squads)
3. Invest in community early (Discord/Slack community for early users)
4. Open-source some components for credibility and developer trust
5. Launch with a waitlist to validate demand before building everything

### Can Defer
1. Enterprise features (SSO, SCIM, audit logs) — add when enterprise asks
2. Advanced automations — start with simple if/then workflows
3. AI features — can launch without AI and add later
4. Mobile apps — responsive web-first, native apps later
5. Third-party integrations — start with Slack + GitHub, add more based on demand

## What Could Change This to NO-GO

| Scenario | Likelihood | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Major incumbent launches identical model | Low (15%) | Would need to pivot to niche |
| AI infrastructure costs spike 5x+ | Very Low (5%) | Cap AI features, adjust pricing |
| Can't build MVP to acceptable quality | Low (10%) | Pivot to simpler tool or different market |
| Zero traction after 6 months of sales | Medium (20%) | Assess product-market fit, pivot or kill |

## Final Word

This is a **high-conviction, low-risk bet**. The downside is bounded (you lose 3-6 months and ~$5K in infra costs). The upside is a genuinely differentiated product in a $10B market with no one doing what you're proposing. The hardest part isn't the pricing model — it's building a PM tool good enough that people want to use it regardless of price. Focus there.
